Emergency First Aid Guide

1. CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

CPR is a life-saving technique useful in emergencies such as heart attack or near drowning, where someone's breathing or heartbeat has stopped.

  • Check for responsiveness and breathing.
  • Call emergency services.
  • Begin chest compressions: Push hard and fast in the center of the chest.
  • Provide rescue breaths if trained to do so.

2. Choking

If someone is choking and cannot breathe, speak, or cough:

  • Perform the Heimlich maneuver: Stand behind the person and give abdominal thrusts.
  • If the person becomes unconscious, begin CPR.

Visual Guide

3. Bleeding

For severe bleeding:

  • Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or bandage.
  • Elevate the injured area if possible.
  • Seek medical help immediately.

Visual Guide

4. Burns

For minor burns:

  • Cool the burn under running water for at least 10 minutes.
  • Cover the burn with a sterile, non-adhesive bandage.
  • Avoid applying ice or butter to the burn.

For severe burns:

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Do not remove burnt clothing.
  • Cover the burn with a clean cloth or bandage.

Visual Guide

5. Fractures

If you suspect a fracture:

  • Immobilize the injured area.
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling.
  • Seek medical attention immediately.

Visual Guide

6. Poisoning

If someone has ingested poison:

  • Call emergency services or a poison control center immediately.
  • Do not induce vomiting unless instructed by a professional.
  • Provide information about the poison if known.

Visual Guide

7. Seizures

If someone is having a seizure:

  • Protect the person from injury by moving objects away.
  • Do not restrain the person or put anything in their mouth.
  • Time the seizure and call emergency services if it lasts longer than 5 minutes.

Visual Guide

8. Heat Stroke

For heat stroke:

  • Move the person to a cooler environment.
  • Apply cool, wet cloths to the skin or immerse in cool water.
  • Seek medical help immediately.

Visual Guide

9. Hypothermia

For hypothermia:

  • Move the person to a warm environment.
  • Remove wet clothing and cover with warm blankets.
  • Seek medical help immediately.

Visual Guide

10. Allergic Reactions

For severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis):

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Use an epinephrine auto-injector if available.
  • Keep the person calm and monitor their breathing.
